Countywide Property Tax Collection Rate Shrouds South Suburban Financial Woes

Editor’s Note: This is the first in a series of periodic analyses, observations and insights into Cook County’s property tax system in a newsletter format produced by Treasurer Maria Pappas’ Office. To subscribe to this newsletter, submit your email here.

Introduction

The historically high rate of property tax collections in Cook County held steady into 2023, but that overall high collection rate masks a little-known fact: collections in many economically struggling, mostly minority south suburbs are perilously low.
Those collection rates, registering below 52% in four suburbs, make it tough for schools, municipalities and other government agencies to provide basic services. For example, in Ford Heights where the collection rate was 29% compared to the countywide rate of 96% has not been able to afford a police department for years.
The lack of collections in those communities also creates pressure for public officials to increase the amounts they ask for, which can push some of the highest tax rates in the nation to even more stratospheric levels.
Another key finding after examining collections for bills mailed last year 25 days after they were due on Dec. 30: collection of property taxes on vacant properties, many of which likely have been abandoned, stood below 70%.

Background

The Treasurer’s Office is charged with billing and collecting property taxes for all local taxing districts in Cook County. For the 2021 tax year, for which taxes are collected in 2022, those taxing bodies sought a total of $16.7 billion in property taxes.
Those bills, and the disparate impact they had on taxpayers in different areas of the county, were analyzed by the Treasurer’s Office in early December, when the second-installment bills were mailed. Payment on those bills was due Dec. 31, 2022.
.
This analysis looks at how much of that money was collected 25 days after the bills were due and compared those collections to previous years at the same point in the collection cycle.
It’s worth noting that past history indicates that collections will continue to rise in coming months, particularly in the weeks before the tax sale which can over time result in the loss of one’s property that is held about 13 months after the due date.

Countywide Overview

As of January 25, 2023, Cook County had collected an estimated 95.9% of property taxes billed for tax year 2021, which were due on December 31, 2022.
The 2021 tax bill collection rate is in line with those of prior years. Collection rates 25 days after the second-installment tax bills were due in the previous four years were all between 95% and 96% (Table 1).

As noted above, collection rates usually increase in the months and years after the bills are due, as delinquent taxes are paid or eventually sold at tax sales. The collection rates for prior years to date currently stand at 98% to 99% countywide.

The 25-day collection rates reflect the amount of money collected for each taxing district in the county, minus the amount refunded for various reasons, including bounced checks, duplicate payments and overpayments.The tax year 2021 collection rates for most types of property, including commercial and industrial properties, apartment buildings, condominiums and single-family homes, were 95% or higher. The major exception was vacant land, on which only 71% of the money billed had been collected 70% (Table 2).
Historically, vacant land has had a much higher likelihood of becoming tax delinquent and being offered at the Annual Tax Sale or the last-ditch Scavenger Sale for chronically deqlinquent properties. About 63% of properties offered at the 2022 Scavenger Sale were vacant lots, even though vacant lots account for only 3.6% of the county’s taxable properties, according to a Treasurer’s Office study.
Countywide, commercial and industrial collections have slightly lower collection rates than residential and multi-family properties. Incentive properties which pay taxes on a lower percentage of their assessed values have higher collection rates than non-incentive properties.

South s uburban municipalities have the lowest collection rates . For the 2021 tax year, Harvey, Robbins, and Phoenix collected between 59.7% and 51.8% of what they billed (Table 4). Ford Heights has by far the county’s lowest collection rate an alarming 29.1%.
These collection rates are driven down by the high amount of chronically vacant and abandoned properties in these communities. In Harvey, the collection rate on vacant lots is 27.8%, and in Ford Heights it is 6.1%.

Most municipalities have had similar collection rates for the most recent five tax years, with those rates fluctuating by fewer than 3 percentage points . The two outliers include Dixmoor, which has seen collection rates increase from an estimated 67% to 78% since 2017, and Ford Heights, which has had its collection rate fall by 9%.
Municipalities with low collection rates struggle to maintain basic local services. In Robbins, a village of 4,600, there were 23 water main breaks in 2022. The mayor sought state and federal aid, saying: “We haven’t wrongfully neglected our infrastructure. We just never have had the money to repair it.”
Government leaders in Harvey, the largest city in Cook County with a collection rate lower than 80%, laid off firefighters to make required payments to its public safety pension fund.

While various community areas struggle to pay property taxes, the city of Chicago’s overll collection rate is still high, so these communities are not in a precarious financial situation like low-income municipalities. Still, high delinquency rates cause a myriad of issues within these community areas, including a high number of properties being offered at tax and scavenger sales and high rates of abandonment.
The chronic delinquency in southern Cook County and Chicago’s South and West sides is tied to a long history of racism, segregation and disinvestment in the Chicago area, explored in the office’s 2022 study “Maps of Inequality: From Redlining to Urban Decay and the Black Exodus.”

Conclusion

Tax collections for tax year 2021 are steady countywide, but in municipalities with concentrated poverty, abandoned properties, and tax delinquency, collection rates are concerningly low. [DO WE HAVE ANY SUGGESTIONS OR POSSIBLE POLICY PLUGS?]
